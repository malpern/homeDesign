Concept 02

Scandinavian Minimalist

Design Goals

Strip the room back to its essentials and let the beautiful architecture speak for itself. Inspired by Nordic design principles of light, simplicity, and function, this concept replaces all heavy furnishings with light oak wood pieces and clean-lined upholstery in soft neutral tones. The bookshelf becomes an exercise in restraint—curated to just a few meaningful objects. A single globe pendant replaces the chandelier. The goal is to create a bright, calm sanctuary that feels spacious and serene, with just a touch of green from carefully placed plants.

Moody Library Lounge

Design Goals

An emerald green velvet Chesterfield sofa and burgundy armchair bring rich jewel tones, while brass accents—chandelier, sconces, side tables—add warmth and glamour. Thick velvet curtains and warm pool lighting make this the ultimate evening retreat for reading, conversation, or a whiskey by the fire.

Concept 04

California Bohemian

Design Goals

Bring the relaxed, sun-drenched energy of California indoor-outdoor living into the space. Natural and organic materials take center stage—rattan, jute, live-edge wood, woven textiles, and clay. The room becomes a botanical sanctuary with lush indoor plants throughout. Earth tones of terracotta, amber, olive, and cream replace the current cool palette. A rattan peacock chair and woven pendant light add sculptural interest, while layered rugs and oversized linen cushions create an inviting, lived-in comfort. The goal is a space that feels like a warm embrace—relaxed, creative, and deeply personal.
